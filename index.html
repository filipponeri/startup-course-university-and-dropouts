What this course is

Startup Foundations for Marie Curie Alumni and selected PhDs is a free online intensive and interactive workshop covering customer discovery, minimum viable product, what YC looks for, while you write a draft accelerator application for your startup idea in five consecutive sessions.

The workshop follows Paul Graham's approach, adapted for people with research expertise. The format is designed for Marie Curie alumni and PhDs who want a structured sprint to a draft YC application. The workshop sessions are not a series of academic lectures, they require instead your active participation doing the pre-session assignments and sharing ideas with the other participants during the session while at the same time polishing your startup idea and application. .

The course is free. There is no equity ask, no upsell, and no commercial angle for participants.

There is no point in attending a session unless, one has completed the pre-session assignment. Therefore completion of the given assignments is required to attend the following session. Another requirement is one's active participation during the session. You will be excluded from the following sessions if you cannot keep up with completing the assignments or with showing an active participation during the sessions.

Who it is for

  • You completed a Marie Curie fellowship at least one year ago. Alternatively, you graduate from a PhD program at least one year ago and you have at least one year of research or work experience in a country different from your native one.
  • You already decided that you want to create a startup and you have an idea to explore by interacting with potential customers.
  • You can attend Thursdays 18:00–20:00 CET for five consecutive weeks from September 10
  • Completing three customer discovery interviews before Session 1 is required for attending Session 1. Admitted applicants will receive a guide on how to conduct the interviews.
  • Completing the tasks assigned at the end of each session is a requirement for attention the following session.
  • You do not need technical co-founders, a business plan, or prior business experience
  • The course is not focused toward applying to European incubators as they are passionate about formal documentation over helping with finding product market fit.

Frequently asked questions

What if I have not done any customer interviews before?

That is fine. The pre-work guide walks you through how to ask for an interview, what questions to ask, and what to listen for. The Mom Test principle — ask about their life, not your idea — is simple enough to apply on your first attempt. Most people find their first interview easier than they expected.

Is there an equity or fee requirement?

No. The course is free. There is no equity ask and no fee. You will not be asked to join any program, fund, or organization as a condition of participating.

Do I need a startup idea to apply?

Yes, you do; you need to know what is you want to focus on your time and energy and what your incubator application will be about. However, it is not necessary to have a fully polished idea. The pre-work interviews are designed to help you find or sharpen the startup idea before Session 1.

What if I do not complete the assignments given before a session? what if I miss a session? What if I do not feel like to actively participate in the session conversation?

In this case, you will be excluded from attending the remaining sessions. Each assignment and each sesssion feeds directly into the next ones, and are required in order to refine your startup concept and incubator application. If your schedule does not allow you to commit the time and effort for attending the workshop, please do not apply.

Is this only for fellows who want to apply to YC specifically?

No. The YC application is the course's structured output — a concrete artifact that forces clear thinking about your company. You can apply to YC, to another accelerator, or to nothing at all. The application is a tool for sharpening your thinking, not a commitment to any particular path.

My fellowship was more than 10 years ago. Can I still apply?

Yes, there is no upper limit. The one year minimum exists to ensure participants have had time to experience and think about the question: 'who do I want to become now?'. Choosing an entrepreneurial path is an answer, choosing an academic career is another one.

I am a current MC fellow / PhD student, or I completed my fellowship/phd less than one year ago. Can I still apply?

No, you cannot apply. The one year minimum exists to ensure participants have had time to decide they really want to become a startup founder.

What time zone is 18:00–20:00 CET?

18:00–20:00 Central European Time is 12:00–14:00 US Eastern Time, and 09:00–11:00 US Pacific Time. The course is EU-primary in its scheduling. US-based participants on the East Coast can join; West Coast participants will need to join at 9am local time.

I was not selected. What happens to my application?

With your permission, your application will be considered for the next available cohort. We will contact you when applications open again.
